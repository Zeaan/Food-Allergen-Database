About Food Allergens

Kidney bean (Phaseolus vulgaris), a common source of plant protein, has been found to sensitize 22% of the food-allergic population in Delhi [phytohaemagglutinin - phaseolin (major)] Causes type-1 hypersensitivity with severe reactions in GI tract. Exhibits cross reactivity with peanut, lentils, black gram, pea etc. The allergen is a glycoprotein, and has a highly stable epitope Black gram is one of the major staple foods in India, and 1.7% of nasobronchial allergy patients were found to be sensitized to black gram extracts. Glycoprotein (28 kDa) displays partial sequence homology to Rho-specific inhibitor of transcription termination Rice (Oryza sativa) is the largest grown staple crop in India and showed a 12% positive cutaneous test within the food-allergic population.
